--- layout: about title: Brian Galitz About Me... ---

I was an Education major when I began my career in healthcare. Partnering with clients to value their inventory, identify expiring product and recalls.

This included pharmacies, long-term care facilities and surgery centers.

After taking a position within the OR's at Northwestern, I realized what a dynamic environment the Supply Chain was. I was able to take ownership over problems that I cared about, affect change and advance our organizational goals. Realizing there was a deficit between the technical team and the operational teams, I immediately changed my major to Computer Science and went to work solving departmental challenges.

I helped develop a tissue-tracking system to meet Joint Commission requirements.

The success of this system helped gain leadership support to expand into a barcode tracking system I designed to manage tissue, high-dollar and consignment inventory.

After which, we partnered with an RFID vendor to develop an automated system to manage supplies and implants which allowed us to reduce inventory ($3M), eliminate expiring product ($200K per quarter) and create labor efficiencies (reduced labor by 25% while expanding into a half dozen ).

I oversaw the expansion of the following:

  • Olson Surgery Center (10 OR's + 1 Anesthesia + 1 SPD + 1 Pre-op/Recovery)
  • Prentice Women's hospital (10 OR's + 4 L&D suites + 1 SPD + 1 Pre-Op/Recovery)
  • The Lavin Pavilion (16 OR's + 2 SPD's + 2 Pre-Op/Recovery + 2 Anesthesia)
  • Grayslake Surgery Center (4 OR's + 1 Pre-Op/Recovery + 1 Anesthesia + 1 SPD)
  • Northwestern IR (8 suites), Cath (3 suites), EP Lab (3 Suites)
